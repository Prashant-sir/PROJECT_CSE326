What is Renewable Energy?
Why Do We Need It?

Renewable energy is essential for ensuring a sustainable and cleaner future. Unlike fossil fuels, which are finite and contribute to climate change, renewable energy sources are abundant, cleaner, and can help power the world for generations. Hereâ€™s why we need renewable energy:

1. Environmental Protection

Renewable energy sources produce little to no greenhouse gas emissions, helping to mitigate climate change and reduce air pollution. They also preserve natural resources, unlike fossil fuels which are finite and cause environmental damage.

2. Energy Independence and Security

Relying on local renewable energy sources reduces dependence on foreign energy imports. This increases energy security and resilience, while reducing vulnerability to geopolitical conflicts and supply disruptions.

3. Economic Growth and Job Creation

The renewable energy sector is creating new jobs in research, development, manufacturing, and installation, offering long-term savings and economic growth, particularly in rural areas with abundant renewable resources.

4. Sustainability for Future Generations

Unlike fossil fuels, which are running out, renewable energy sources like wind, solar, and water will last for as long as the Earth does, providing a reliable and sustainable energy solution for future generations.

5. Health Benefits

Renewable energy reduces air pollution, improving public health by decreasing respiratory and cardiovascular diseases. It also reduces water usage, unlike fossil fuel plants that consume large amounts of water for cooling.

6. Combatting Climate Change

By reducing carbon emissions, renewable energy plays a crucial role in addressing global warming. It helps countries meet international climate goals like the Paris Agreement and is essential for building a sustainable future.

Different types of Renewable Energies

Solar Energy

"Solar power is the last energy resource that isn't owned yet - nobody taxes the sun yet.
Every 24 hours, enough sunlight touches the Earth to provide energy for the entire planet for 24 years.
Solar power is going to be essential to meeting growing energy demands while staving off climate change.

Wind Energy

Wind energy is the process of using wind to generate electricity or mechanical power.
Wind turbines use the kinetic energy of moving air to turn blades that spin a shaft connected to a generator to produce electricity

Hydroelectric Energy

A hydroelectric turbine is a machine that converts the kinetic and potential energy of water into mechanical energy,
which is then used to generate electricity

Biomass

Biomass energy is a renewable energy source that comes from plants and animals,
and can be used to generate electricity and other products:
Examples:
Wood, agricultural residues, energy crops, and organic waste from households and industry

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy is heat energy that comes from the Earth's core and can be used for a variety of purposes,
including heating, cooling, and generating electricity

Oceanic Thermal Energy

Ocean thermal energy conversion (OTEC) is a renewable energy technology
that uses the temperature difference between the ocean's warm surface water and cold depths to generate electricity

Oceanic Tidal Energy

Tidal energy is a renewable energy source that
harnesses the natural rise and fall of tides to generate electricity

Marine Wave Energy

wave power, a form of renewable energy in which electricity is generated by harnessing the up-and-down motion of ocean waves.
Wave power is typically produced by floating turbine platforms or buoys that rise and fall with the swells.
